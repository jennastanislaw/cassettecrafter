What is Golden Gate Assembly?

Golden Gate Assembly is a molecular cloning method that enables the assembly of DNA fragments in a seamless and efficient way. This technique takes advantage of Type IIS restriction enzymes to cut DNA outside their recognition sites, allowing for precise joining of DNA fragments with custom overhangs.

Advantages: It allows for the simultaneous and directional assembly of multiple fragments without leaving unwanted restriction sites.

This software generates a library of DNA sequence cassettes that contains all possible combinations of allowed mutations at specified positions, with the necessary recognition sequences for Golden Gate Assembly. Enzymes, such as BssI, recognize specific DNA sequences but cut at a site outside their recognition sequence. This unique property enables the creation of DNA fragments with custom overhangs. When these overhangs are designed to be complementary, they guide the assembly of DNA fragments in a specific order during a ligation reaction.
